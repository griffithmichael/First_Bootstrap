Fincher's Top 4

Seven

1995

Seven is Fincher's second major film release following the release of Alien 3 in 1993. Seven is a neo-noir drama about a serial killer who exacts his murders inline with the seven deadly sins. Seven stars: Brad Pitt, Morgan Freeman, Gwenyth Paltrow, and Kevin Spacey

Fight Club

1999

Based on the 1996 novel of the same name by Chuck Palahniuk, Fight Club follows protagonist Tyler Durden as he creates an underground fighting circuit in an attempt to escape the mundane routine of his everyday life. Fight Club stars: Edward Norton, Bradd Pitt, and Helena Bonham Carter

The Social Network

2010

The Social Network Score was provided by Atticus Ross and Trent Reznor (Nine Inch Nails)

The above song is not actually from the soundtrack but from royalty free artist Ben Sound

Based on the 2009 autobiography - "The Accidental Billionaires: The Founding of Facebook, a Tale of Sex, Money, Genius, and Betrayal" by Ben Mezrich, The Social Network recounts how Mark Zuckerberg created Facebook and the issues surrounding its creation. The Social Network stars: Jesse Eisenberg, Andrew Garfield, and Justin Timberlake

Gone Girl

2014

Based on the 2012 novel of the same name by Gillian Flynn, Gone Girl tells the story of Nick Dunne a struggling writing teacher who struggles under the scrutiny of the public eye when his wife - Amy - disappears. All signs point towards Nick murdering his wife and he must fight against the media and the law in an attempt to find Amy. Gone Girl stars: Ben Affleck, Rosamund Pike, Neil Patrick Harris, and Tyler Perry
